Foundation Reflections

Maximilon Baddeley

Thu 16 Jul 2:00PM

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

Ihave learned that I can keep to a schedule if I remain focused and disciplined on the task at hand. However, I have also learned that if I want to do my best work I need to drive and push myself through apathetic moments and take control. I’m a little older than I once was and as a result my ego is rather in harmony these days. I celebrate the potential in others to grow. We all have our moments.

What are the role of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I’m not too sure how to answer this objectively. Learning to program is quite an individual experience. I first learned to program because I was fascinated with how code was written. I did not meet anybody who could code for a long time in my life, but I suspect that If I had I would of taken to it quite strongly growing up. When I first learned JavaScript I found it incredibly difficult. With time however, I learned that certain benchmarks such as logical operations and control flows, and so on, is more like philosophy than mathematics. Values are therefore subjective to the desire for code. I wanted to learn how to build things in modern ways.

Self-awareness in learning is absolutely more of a guide in how I learn. If I have a learning objective, then I will persist and explore that space until I am satisfied with the knowledge gained. If you are constantly focused on your own learning however than the learning of others may be neglected as a result. Empathy may therefore contribute to celebrating the learning journey of others and provide a way to relate to the network of likeminded individuals as a whole. I’ve absolutely met passionate programmers before and if people tend to take more positive outlooks toward building things than turning quick profits, then perhaps more things could get made.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The lack of ES6. I was expecting more modern updates on older ways of doing things I am being completely honest.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found the algorithm challenges the hardest. There are so many ways to solve problems in JavaScript. I really needed to hit the books to remember a lot of key terminology and techniques to accomplish the tasks successfully.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

There are simply fundamental skills required for any programming task. Without the code knowledge, the bootcamp may run at a pace unforgiving to students who lack the fundamentals.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

I felt that half of the core was good whilst half was a bit of a time sink. I actually used that time to practice programming before looking at the challenges. I will spend the next week continuing to explore and study JavaScript. Again this has to do with the lack of ES6 content. I understand how an absolute beginner may struggle with this however.
