Reflecting on how I see emotional intelligence

Maximilon Baddeley

Fri 2 Jul 2:00PM

Emotional intelligence is often contrasted with intellectual intelligence. My understanding of intelligence is probably less binary of a definition and more of a holistic awareness that intelligence is a measurement rather than an entity. For example, being contextually aware is more of a sign of intelligence for me than any other metric. If I can see how people respond to the immediate context that they find themselves within I am able to make judgements on the intelligence of their actions. In this way, intelligence is a practical action.

Empathy seems to be a keyword passed around these days. How empathic somebody is toward someone else could be a sign of emotional intelligence. Yet there may also be a consideration worthy for further discussion here. Boundaries, for instance, play a role in allowing empathy to not venture too far. Having strong boundaries also may require strong communication and relationship building skills. Emotional intelligence is also part of this side of the coin.

Ultimately, emotional intelligence could be valued when working with others. As we are increasingly finding flexible means to collaborate, having somebody who can recognise the opportunities to connect with others and build local cultures is a sure sign of intelligence, regardless of how you would like to define it.
